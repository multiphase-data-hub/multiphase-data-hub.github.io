Data standard

Metadata that makes multiphase-flow data reusable.

Every dataset should describe the physics, numerics or experiment, file structure, units, splits, provenance, license, and citation.

Physics

Flow configuration, phases, material properties, dimensionless groups, boundary conditions, and governing assumptions.

Numerics

Solver, discretization, grid, interface method, time stepping, convergence notes, and known limitations.

Experiment

Measurement method, calibration, uncertainty, camera or probe settings, acquisition rate, and post-processing.

AI readiness

Array names, shapes, units, coordinate order, splits, preprocessing, baselines, and evaluation metrics.

Provenance

Authors, institution, software version, generation scripts, hardware notes, release version, and checksums.

Reuse terms

License, citation, DOI or persistent URL, contact person, and restrictions for redistribution or commercial use.

Recommended formats

Use common scientific formats with documented loaders.

HDF5 / Zarr

Best for large multidimensional fields and time-resolved data.

NPZ

Good for compact AI-ready tensors and small benchmarks.

VTK / XDMF / EnSight

Useful for CFD field outputs and visualization workflows.

CSV / Parquet

Appropriate for diagnostics, scalar metrics, and case summaries.
